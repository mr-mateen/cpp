What is Cyber Security?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting critical systems and sensitive information from digital attacks. It's also known as information technology security or electronic information security. cybersecurity measures are designed to combat threats against networked systems and applications, whether those threats originate from inside or outside of an organization.

A strong cybersecurity strategy has layers of protection to defend against cyber crime, including cyber attacks that attempt to access, change, or destroy data; extort money from users or the organization; or aim to disrupt normal business operations. Countermeasures should address

Network Security

Security measures for protecting a computer network from intruders, including both wired and wireless connections.

Application Security

Focuses on keeping software and devices free of threats. A compromised application could provide access to the data it's designed to protect stored on local machine or in cloud

Cloud Security

Works with confidential computing that encrypts cloud data at rest, in motion (accessed through application), and in use data (during processing), to support customer privacy, business requirements.

Information Security

Protects the integrity and privacy of data, both in storage and in transit.

Operational Security

It includes the process and decisons for handling and protecting data assets. The permissions user's have when accessing a network and the procedures that determine how and wher data may be stores and accessed, all fall under ths umbrella.

End-User Education

It addresses the most unpredicable cyber-secutiry factor. Anyone can accidentally introduce a virus in the system by failing the good security practices. Teaching users to delete or intall suspecious applications, plugin unidentified USB drives and other vaious important lesson to bypass the system secutiry and gaining access to the protected system.

Disaster Recover and business continuity

It defines how an orginization respondes to a cyber-security incident or any other event that may casue user to face downtime of the service. Disaster recovery policies dictate how the data is orginization restores the operations and information to return to the same operating capacity as before the the event

Storage Secutiry

This includes encryption and immutable and isolated data copes. These remain in the same pool so they can quickly be restores to support recovery, minimizing the impact of a cyer attack

The Scale of the Cyber Threat

The global cyber threat continues to evolve at a rapid place, with a rising number of data breaches each year. A report revealed that a shocking 7.9 billion records have been expose by data breaches in the first nine months of 2019 alone. This figure is more than double the number of records exposed in the same period in 2018.

With the scale of the cyber threat set to continue to rise, global spending on cyber-security solutions is naturally increasing. Governments across the globe have responded to the rising cyber threat with gudance to help orginizations implement effective cyber-security practices.

How malicious individuals gain control over system?

Malware:

Malware means malicious software, designed by hacker to disrupt or damage the victims computer or system. Often spread via email attachment or legitimate-looking download

Ransomware:

is a type of malware that locks down files, data94 or systems, and threatens to erase or destroy the data - or make private or sensitive data to the public - unless a ransom is paid to the cyber criminals who launched the attack.

Phishing / social engineering:

Phishing is a form of social-engineering that tricks the user to provide their own sensitive information. In phishing scams, emails or text messages appear to form a legitimate company asking for sensitive information, such as credit card data or login credentials
